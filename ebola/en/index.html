OUR MISSION To block the spread of ÉBOLA, SOLIDARITÉS INTERNATIONAL’s mission is to
decontaminate the centre and protect the medical staff.

  • managing the supplies of drinking water and chlorinated water
  • Disinfecting medical equipment
  • Decontaminating the center on a daily basis
  • Managing waste, especially medical waste
  • Managing laundry services
  • Taking care of the bodies of deceased patients

WITH NO KNOWN CURE FOR THE DISEASE, OUR EXPERTISE IN WATER, HYGIENE AND SANITATION IS CRUCIAL TO STOPPING THE EPIDEMIC AND SAVING THOUSANDS OF LIVES.

Managing water supplies

Water supply and chlorination: a minimum of 150 litres of water per patient per day (drinking water, water for cleaning and disinfection).
Maintenance of water networks for the 500 staff and patients at the centre.

Decontamination

Waste management (waste-water, solid and medical waste, protective suits…) and incineration.
Laundry services.
Disinfection of toilets and showers.
Decontamination of the whole centre on a daily basis: 1kg of chlorine per bed per day.

Ensuring safe burials

Taking care of the bodies of deceased patients, which are particularly contagious.
Informing families that contact with the body of an EBOLA victim is very dangerous.
Ensuring dignified burials.

OUR CENTRE SOLIDARITÉS INTERNATIONAL’s expertise is at the heart of the fight against the epidemic.

La Sierra Leone,in the midst of the epidemic

Moyamba
Southern province
18 000 inhabitants ,
250 000 inhabitants in the district
No hospital facilities able to care for ÉBOLA patients.

When it opens on December 17th, the Moyamba centre will be the only hospital structure providing care for EBOLA patients in the region.

Initial capacity: 50 beds
Extension planned in January 2015
Total capacity: 100 beds

The Moyamba Ebola Treatment Centre is an essential link in the fight against the epidemic. It is here that our team is being deployed.
